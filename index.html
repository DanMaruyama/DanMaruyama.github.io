About

I am a machine learning scientist and data scientist living in the Bay Area.
Previously I was a physics doctoral student in the Zochowski lab at the Univeristy of Michigan. My research primarily focused on measuring the functional relationships between neurons and studying how these functional networks evolve in time. The goal of my research was to understand how changes in functional connectivity achieves underlying neuronal function.

Interests

My interests include network-level encoding, cortex algorithms, the bio-physical mechanisms of neuronal algorithms, and golf... golf is great! I particularly enjoy looking for systems neuroscience insight from machine learning and information theory.
